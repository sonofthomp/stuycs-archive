StuyCS Electives Archive

Hello!

This website is meant to archive the notes and coursework for senior-year elective CS courses at Stuyvesant.

The Computer Science department has recently decided that students will not longer be able to take more than one CS elective at a time, due to limits on the number of seats in these classes. This website is intended to allow students to study the coursework from classes other than the one they're taking, if they desire to.


Recommendations for studying:

  • The notes section for all three of the courses are utterly invaluable
  • Don't just read and the "notes" section and expect to understand it. Actively experiment with everything mentioned in the notes, to solidify it in your memory.
  • Keep a document for taking notes. For each lesson, re-phrase/re-organize what is written in the "notes" section in a way that helps you understand it best. This process of paraphrasing will force you to internalize the information, and how you reorganize it will make it easier for you to access later on.
  • Pace yourself. Even if an earlier assignment feels excessively easy, it's important to still spend an adequate amount of time on it because the rest of the course will require that you have a rock-solid understanding of it.
  • Put as much thought and effort into assignments as you would with an APCS assignment
